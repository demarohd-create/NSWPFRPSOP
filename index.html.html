Arrest Caution

My name is [rank] [name] from the NSW Police Force.

You are under arrest for [offence].

You do not have to say or do anything, but anything you say or do may be used as evidence in a court of law.

You have the right to contact a friend or family member to let them know your whereabouts.

You also have the right to speak with a lawyer. If you do not have one, one can be appointed to you.

Do you understand this caution?

If no response is received, the caution must be read again.

Use of Force & Tackling
Use of force (quick)
  • Start verbal & de-escalate where possible.
  • Use the least force needed; escalate only if resistance/assault increases.
  • Once controlled: cuff, search, provide medical if required.
Tackling (procedure)
  • Call it: “Tackling!” Ensure it’s safe (no traffic/edges).
  • Aim low (hips/legs), control arms, move to cuffs.
  • Update comms: location, status, injuries.

Pursuit Quick Guide

Radio Crib

"[Callsign], pursuit on [vehicle], traffic [L/M/H], weather [clear/rain], heading [direction] on [road], hostilities [yes/no], requesting backup."

Authorisations

0–5 Minutes: No PITs. No spike strips. Containment / tracking only.
5+ Minutes: PIT under 100 km/h automatically authorised.
10+ Minutes: PIT under 175 km/h automatically authorised. Spike strips may be deployed.
Hostile Pursuit (timers void): Ramming / assisting vehicle / shots fired. PIT under 175 km/h permitted. Spikes / barricades permitted.

Only PD Command or Highway may approve PITs, boxes, or spikes. Supervisor approval is not required to initiate a pursuit.

Promotions

Promotions are available to all ranks once a member has completed their probationary stage.

Promotions are assessed on:

  • Good quality RP and professionalism
  • Activity/attendance and reliability
  • Decision-making and handling of situations
  • Communication, teamwork, and leadership potential

Note: Meeting the above does not guarantee promotion — it supports eligibility and consideration.

Vehicle & Division Policy
  • Officers must operate vehicles assigned to their division.
  • Highway Patrol vehicles require Highway Command certification.
  • Bike Patrol requires certification and a minimum of 4 officers on duty.
  • PolAir requires certification and a minimum of 3 officers on duty.
  • Improper vehicle usage may result in disciplinary action.
Legal Standards

Probable Cause

A reasonable belief, supported by facts and circumstances, that a crime has been committed.

Reasonable Suspicion

Specific and articulable facts which indicate that criminal activity may be occurring.

Search Procedures

  • All suspects must be searched upon arrest.
  • Suspects must be searched again post-hospital release.

Officer Discretion

Officers may exercise discretion in minor cases and issue citations or warnings where appropriate.

Scene Management
  • Upon arrival, officers must relay suspects, weapons, hostages, and vehicle descriptions.
  • A minimum of 3 officers is required to authorise a breach.
  • The highest ranking officer on scene authorises breach decisions.
  • Officers must not identify themselves by voice during tactical operations.
  • Clear and controlled communication must be maintained at all times.
Radio Protocol
  • 10-3 must be used to stop radio chatter during priority incidents.
  • EMS must stage until Code 4 is declared.
  • Officers are not to self-attach to pursuits without authorisation.
  • Clear callsigns must be used at all times.
  • Radio discipline is mandatory during active scenes and pursuits.
MDT & Reporting
  • All officers involved must be listed.
  • All suspects and civilians must be documented.
  • Evidence must be properly logged.
  • Weapons and confiscated items must be recorded.
  • No additional charges may be added after sentencing.
  • Maximum sentence: 60 months (High Command approval required).
  • Warrants must be filed within 7 days.
  • Search warrants are restricted to Detectives and AFP.

Professional Conduct & Strike System
  • Chain of command must be respected at all times.
  • A strike remains active for 2 weeks.
  • A further breach during an active strike may result in suspension.
  • Officers must maintain professionalism at all times.
  • Bodycam and dashcam usage is mandatory.
  • Suspects must be transported to PD immediately unless unsafe.
  • GSR testing is permitted during shots fired investigations.
K-9 Unit Standard Operating Procedures

Purpose

This SOP establishes guidelines for the deployment, care, handling, training, and documentation related to police K-9 units.

Roles & Responsibilities

  • K-9 Handler: Maintains control of the K-9, ensures care, health checks, training, and completes deployment documentation.
  • K-9 Supervisor: Oversees assignments and training, approves deployments, reviews reports.
  • Veterinary Support: Provides medical care and maintains vaccination and health records.

Training Requirements

  • Certified initial training
  • Ongoing weekly/monthly training sessions
  • Annual recertification
  • Maintenance of training records

K-9 Care & Welfare

  • Daily feeding and hydration
  • Safe kennel and housing arrangements
  • Regular exercise requirements
  • Reporting injuries or illness immediately
  • Climate-controlled transport at all times

Deployment Guidelines

  • Handler supervision is mandatory
  • Consistent commands must be used
  • Legal justification is required prior to deployment
  • K-9s may be used to prevent escape, protect officers, or stop threats
  • Verbal warnings should be given when feasible
  • Detection dogs require lawful authority and documented alerts

Use of Force

  • K-9 deployment is considered a use of force
  • Medical assistance must be provided after any bite
  • All deployments are subject to supervisor review

Recordkeeping

  • Deployment logs
  • Training logs
  • Veterinary records
  • Bite / use-of-force reports
  • Equipment maintenance logs

Equipment Standards

  • Approved harnesses, leashes, and muzzles
  • Ventilated vehicle kennels
  • Tracking and detection equipment
  • First aid kits

Public Demonstrations

  • Command approval required
  • Safety perimeters must be established
  • No public participation in apprehension demonstrations

Retirement & Reassignment

  • Determined by health and performance
  • Adoption procedures for handlers or families
  • Ownership transfer must be documented
